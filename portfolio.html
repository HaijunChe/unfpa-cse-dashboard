PROJECT 02

Air Quality & Health Risk Monitoring

Identifying non-obvious environmental signals as health risk indicators for data-scarce populations

Seasonal Pattern Analysis

Predictive Lag Analysis (3-week lead)

Comprehensive Environment-ILI Correlation

Model Residual Analysis

This project tracks air quality and meteorological data as early signals for influenza-like illness (ILI) outbreaks in Karachi, a city with limited health surveillance infrastructure. The key finding: temperature and NO₂ levels predict ILI cases with a 3-week lead time (r=-0.419, p=0.0001). This kind of cross-indicator analysis — finding non-obvious linkages that standard reporting would miss — is directly transferable to CSE programme monitoring data.
